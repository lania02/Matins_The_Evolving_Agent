# Taste Skill (cold start)

This document is the human-readable mirror of the **active taste-skill version** --
the slow, consolidated model of what makes a research idea good *for this user*.

**At cold start it is intentionally empty.** The system has not yet observed enough of
your rankings and comments to claim it knows your taste. Nothing here was learned from a
single observation: this file is only ever updated through **human-approved
consolidation** (DESIGN.md section 8), when a structural preference has shown enough
persistence across many days to be worth committing. Each update creates a new version,
so rollback is trivial.

Until then, the placeholder principles below are deliberately neutral and generic. They
exist so the generation prompt has something to condition on, not because they describe
you. Expect them to be replaced as real signal accumulates.

## Placeholder principles (neutral; to be replaced by consolidation)

1. **Prefer concreteness over grandeur.** An idea with an honest first concrete step
   beats a sweeping vision with no entry point.

2. **Novelty is an empirical claim, not a feeling.** An idea is more valuable when its
   relationship to existing work is named, not assumed.

3. **Reward legible structure.** Ideas that expose a clear mechanism or an explicit
   mapping to a known problem are easier to judge and to build on.
