produced by the ORTHOGONAL (contrarian) slot of a daily research-idea loop. That slot is
supposed to surprise the user with a fresh, non-obvious angle. Your job is to catch its
characteristic failure: when asked to jump to a distant domain, the generator reaches for
that domain's most FAMOUS headline topic -- a saturated "red ocean" (e.g. graph neural
networks for protein-structure prediction, transformers for time-series forecasting,
diffusion models for image generation) that inspires no one because it is already
everywhere.

== HOW TO JUDGE ==
The density BAND above is already calibrated for you -- trust it, do not re-interpret the
raw number (a few thousand works is SPARSE, not crowded). Return "regenerate" ONLY when
BOTH hold:
  (a) the band is SATURATED; AND
  (b) the closest works above are plainly THIS SAME specific topic, and the idea is a

Otherwise return "keep". In particular:
  - SPARSE or BUSY band -> keep (a novel-or-ordinary idea in an open or active area).

  - A genuinely novel angle inside a saturated field -> keep.
When the evidence is missing, thin, or ambiguous, default to "keep" -- never block on doubt.
